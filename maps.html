Paris

Paris, France, is a place many people go to on vacation, but I go for a different reason. North of Paris, their is a factory, the Selmer factory. If you go to the Selmer factory, you will be able to buy instruments (bass clarinets in particular) for less than through mail order in the US (including flight cost, food, and housing), like, a couple hundred less, and you can get it adjusted for free, to make it perfect (for you). France is home to other music companys, like Vandoren, LeBlanc, Buffet, and many others I'm sure. So checking those out would be cool too. Plus its Paris so you can get some pretty good food.

New York

New York, New York is home to several things of intrest. the most interesting, to me, is the Vandoren Studio, which has many mouthpieces and ligatures, and reeds that you can try for free, here I would buy a perfect set up for my new bass clarinet. Miles Davis and Dizzy Gillespie are both buried in New York, so you can maybe vist there graves. Typically New York will have some sort of interesting gathering or similar meeting, these could be interesting.
